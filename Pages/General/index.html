General Facility Information

Virtual Reality Headsets


Headset Starting Procedure

  1. Pick up the headset and hold it up to your face
  2. Press and hold the little button located on the right side of the headset, underneath the strap
  3. Hold this button until you see a blue 'META' logo (See Below)
  4. After a minute or two, you will appear to be in a room with furniture and a nice view.
  5. Locate the floating screen by looking around
  6. After another minute or two, a little white circle cursor should appear
  7. In the navigation bar at the bottom of the floating screen, locate a black application with a white 3D cube pictured (See Below)
  8. Aim the white cursor over the top of this app, and click the volume up button
    • Volume buttons are located on the bottom right side of the headset, volume up is the right side of this button and volume down is on the left side of this button.
  9. It will now prompt you to switch to controllers, just aim the cursor over the 'Continue' button and press the volume up button again to click.
  10. You should end up at the start page of the VR experience.

Proximity/Swipe Fobs


Proximity / swipe cards are supplied to new staff at time of orientation. Level of access is determined by an employee's position.

If level of access is required to be changed, then the appropriate General Manager requests a change of access from the Quality Systems Administrator.

Shift Swapping


If staff want to swap shifts they must fill out a shift swap form.

Forms are located with the spare timesheets above the safe.

Shift swap forms should be completed as soon as possible and must be signed off by a manager.

Availabilities


When availabilities change, let the manager know as soon as possible.

The best way to do this is to send an email, detailing the dates/days you would like to not be rostered on. Ideally, this is sent before the next roster is sent out.

Shifts and Timesheets


Timesheets are to b filled out after each completed shift.

They are located above the safe in the red binder. Spares are also located above the safe.

Shift times must be written in decimal format to 2 decimal places.

See the table below to see all available shifts that may be rostered to you.

Working for 5 hours or less there is no break entitlement. Working more than 5 hours you are entitled to a 20 minute paid break. If you want to extend this break to 30 minutes, the last 10 minutes of the break will be unpaid and will have to be deducted from the total shift time.
